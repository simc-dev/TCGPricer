Why this exists

The core loop

A shop-native workflow that stays fast

1) Scan camera capture + extraction

Extracts card identity, then asks you to confirm the details that matter before any price logic runs.

2) Quote multi-source benchmark

Pulls recent listings and produces a benchmark price with transparent notes on why each source did or didn’t count.

3) Gate risk-based approval

Requires manual approval when the quote is high-value or low-confidence, preventing costly mistakes.

4) Save Google Sheets logging

One click appends a clean row: identity, benchmark, buy/sell suggestion, sources, and notes.

Pricing logic

Benchmark from the best available market data

Source priority is explicit and fallback-driven: Carousell → Mercari → Yuyutei (in-stock) → PriceCharting. A benchmark wins only when there are enough clean listings and the signal is stable.

Output: benchmark median-based signal
  • Recent listing window + outlier controls
  • Condition and variant adjustments
  • Notes explain why each listing counted
Output: suggestion buy / sell guardrails
  • Modes: buy / sell / consignment support
  • Rounding rules for quick quoting
  • Confidence reported with ambiguity flags

Safety rails

High-risk quotes require manual approval

Triggers avoid expensive mistakes
  • Benchmark above a high-value threshold (e.g., > 100 SGD)
  • Confidence below target (e.g., < 0.90)
  • Variant ambiguity or incomplete identity confirmation
Result pit of success

The app slows you down only when the situation is risky. Everything else stays fast: confirm, quote, save.
